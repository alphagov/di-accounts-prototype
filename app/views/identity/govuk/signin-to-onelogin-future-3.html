How to prove your identity with GOV.UK One Login

When you prove your identity with GOV.UK One Login, your identity information is saved automatically. This means you won’t have to prove your identity again next time a service needs to check who you are.

If you’ve already proved your identity you can sign in to view and update your saved identity information.

Ways to prove your identity

There are 3 ways of proving your identity with GOV.UK One Login:

  • online with the GOV.UK ID check app
  • online by answering security questions
  • in person at a Post Office

The method you use will depend on your device and which identity documents you have.

Online with the GOV.UK ID check app

You’ll need access to a smartphone or tablet with a camera.

You also need to have one of the following ID documents:

  • UK on non-UK biometric passport
  • UK driving licence

Online by answering security questions

You’ll have to ask some security questions about things like your mobile phone contract, bank accounts, credit cards, loans or mortgages you may have.

You also need to have one of the following ID documents:

  • UK passport
  • UK driving licence

In person at a Post Office

This option takes longer. You’ll usually get an outcome within a day of going to the Post Office.

You’ll be asked to:

  • enter details from your photo ID on GOV.UK
  • go a Post Office where they’ll scan your ID and take a photo of your face

You need to have one of the following ID documents:

  • a passport from any country
  • a UK or EU driving licence with your photo on it
  • a biometric residence permit (BRP)
  • an identity card from a European Economic Area (EEA) country, except Denmark, Iceland or Ireland
